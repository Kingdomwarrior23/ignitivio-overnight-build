# Notebook LM Agency Integration Guide
**For: IgnitivIO SEO/AEO Services**

## Quick Setup (15 minutes)

### Step 1: Access Notebook LM
1. Go to `notebooklm.google.com`
2. Sign in with your Google account (kingdomwarrior23@gmail.com)
3. Click "New Notebook"

### Step 2: Create Your First Agency Notebook
**Name:** "[Client Name] - Competitive Analysis [Date]"

**Sources to upload:**
1. Client's website pages (export from Firecrawl)
2. Top 3 competitor sites (export from Firecrawl)  
3. Industry research (Exa results)
4. Client's current content (if they have a blog)

**How to upload:**
- Text files: Drag and drop
- URLs: Paste directly (it scrapes automatically)
- PDFs: Upload directly
- YouTube videos: Paste URL (it transcribes)

### Step 3: Ask Strategic Questions

**Don't ask:** "What is this about?"
**Do ask:**

**For Content Gap Analysis:**
- "What topics appear on all 3 competitor sites but are missing from [client]'s site?"
- "What questions do competitors answer that [client] doesn't address?"
- "Which pain points get the most attention across competitor content?"

**For Keyword Research:**
- "What specific terms and phrases do competitors use repeatedly when discussing [topic]?"
- "What semantic clusters appear across all competitor content?"
- "What long-tail variations are competitors targeting?"

**For Positioning:**
- "How does each competitor position themselves differently?"
- "What value propositions appear most frequently?"
- "What objections do competitors address in their content?"

**For Content Strategy:**
- "If I had to create 10 pieces of content to fill [client]'s biggest gaps, what topics would have the highest impact based on this competitive analysis?"
- "What content formats are competitors using that [client] isn't? (guides, calculators, case studies, etc.)"

### Step 4: Export to Claude

**Copy Notebook LM's cited answers, then in Claude:**

```
I ran a competitive analysis for [client] in their industry. Here are the key findings with sources:

[Paste Notebook LM answers with citations]

Based on this research, create:
1. A 10-point content strategy prioritized by impact
2. 5 page titles with target keywords
3. A 90-day content calendar
4. Positioning recommendations

Format as a client-ready report with sections, headers, and actionable next steps.
```

## Templates for Common Questions

### Content Gap Template
```
Compare [client]'s content coverage against competitors in these areas:
1. [Topic area 1]
2. [Topic area 2]  
3. [Topic area 3]

For each area, identify:
- What competitors cover that [client] doesn't
- Which competitor covers it best (with examples)
- What the content quality/depth difference is
- Estimated content value/impact
```

### Positioning Template  
```
Analyze how each competitor positions themselves:
- What's their main value proposition?
- What audience do they target?
- What tone/voice do they use?
- What makes them different from others?
- What weaknesses/gaps appear in their positioning?

Then recommend: How should [client] position differently to stand out?
```

### Keyword Strategy Template
```
Extract all target keywords and semantic clusters from competitor content.
Group by:
- Search intent (informational, commercial, transactional)
- Difficulty (based on how many competitors target it)
- Opportunity (topics where competitors are weak)

Recommend the top 10 keyword targets for [client] based on:
- Low competition among this group
- High relevance to [client]'s services
- Clear conversion potential
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Uploading too many sources at once (max 20-30 per notebook)
❌ Asking generic questions ("What is this about?")
❌ Not using the citations in client deliverables
❌ Forgetting to update notebooks with new competitor content
❌ Treating it like Google search (it only knows what you upload)

✅ Keep notebooks focused (one client, one project, one topic)
✅ Ask specific, strategic questions
✅ Always cite sources in client reports
✅ Update notebooks monthly or quarterly
✅ Think of it as your research assistant with a perfect memory
