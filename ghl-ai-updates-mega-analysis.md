### 3. UNLIMITED MESSAGING SYSTEM ($297-497/mo setup + $50-150/mo per client)

**What It Is:**
- Alternative to GHL's A2P (Application-to-Person) SMS
- Uses P2P (Peer-to-Peer) through SendBlue iMessage system
- Unlimited texts and calls without per-message fees
- Pay per phone line, not per message/minute

**Why It Matters:**
- GHL charges per text ($0.01-0.03 each)
- GHL charges per call minute ($0.06-0.16/min)
- High-volume clients = high variable costs
- P2P eliminates variable costs entirely

**SendBlue Pricing Model:**
- Each phone line = 50 new leads/day capacity
- After lead replies once, unlimited follow-ups (free)
- Client with 100 leads/day = 2 lines
- Typical cost: $50-150/mo per client (vs $300-800/mo on A2P)

**Technical Setup:**
1. Install SendBlue extension from Chrome Web Store
2. In GHL, go to Phone System → Additional Settings
3. Switch provider from "Lead Connector" to "SendBlue iMessage"  
4. Activate phone lines with SendBlue (same-day usually)
5. Select SendBlue as SMS provider in workflows
6. Test: Widget popup shows iPhone-style interface
7. Use SendBlue phone widget for voice messages, videos, text effects

**Features:**
- **iMessage first, SMS fallback** - Sends blue bubble to iPhone, green to Android automatically
- **Rich media** - Videos, voice messages, text effects display natively (no links)
- **2x reply rates** - iMessage consistently outperforms SMS
- **Voice messages** - Record and send audio directly from widget
- **Video sharing** - Send video attachments that preview inline
- **No A2P registration** - Skip the carrier compliance nightmare

**Who Needs This:**
- Agencies with high-volume texting (>10K messages/mo)
- Clients doing appointment reminders (auto, dental, med spa)
- Reactivation campaigns (texting old customer lists)
- Event promotion (fitness studios, restaurants)

**Selling Angle:**
"Right now, you're paying $0.015 per text. Last month you sent 15,000 texts—that's $225 just in message fees. I can give you unlimited texting for $97/mo flat. Oh, and iMessage gets 2x the reply rate of regular texts, so you'll actually book MORE appointments while spending LESS."

**Important Limitations:**
- Not for cold outreach or spam
- Best for warm leads who opted in
- Still need A2P for promotional blasts to cold lists
- Contact must be created in GHL first

## ⚠️ COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

### 1. Building Without Testing
**Mistake:** Spend 10 hours building elaborate website and workflows before client pays.

**Fix:** Build 80% solution in 2 hours using AI Studio, show client, get deposit, THEN perfect it.

### 2. Underpricing to "Get Started"
**Mistake:** Charge $297/mo because you want some quick wins.

**Fix:** You can't raise prices easily. Start at $997 minimum. Offer payment plan ($497 setup, then $997/mo) if they balk.

### 3. Not Documenting Your Process
**Mistake:** Reinvent the wheel for every client, forget steps, scramble to remember how you did something.

**Fix:** After building something, immediately Loom record yourself rebuilding it while explaining. Put all Looms in a Google Drive folder. You now have training docs for VAs.

### 4. Trying to Do Everything
**Mistake:** Offer websites, SEO, ads, social, email, content, video, design, strategy, and custom software. Client asks "So what do you do?" and you don't have a clear answer.

**Fix:** Pick 1-2 core offers. For you, I'd suggest:
- Core Offer 1: AI Studio websites + lead gen automation
- Core Offer 2: Ask Maps optimization

Everything else is an upsell after they're already paying you.

### 5. Chasing Random Niches
**Mistake:** One client is a dentist, one is a lawyer, one is a restaurant, one is a SaaS company. You can't reuse anything, every build is custom.

**Fix:** Pick 3 niches max. For Lubbock, I'd go:
- Home services (roofing, HVAC, plumbing, electrical, landscaping)
- Auto services (repair, detailing, towing, glass)
- Med spa / beauty (med spas, salons, aesthetics, weight loss)

Why these three?
- All local service-based (not e-commerce, not complicated)
- All have similar workflows (quotes, appointments, reviews)  
- All have similar pain points (leads, no-shows, reviews)
- You can reuse 90% of your builds across them

### 6. No Client Onboarding Process
**Mistake:** Client pays you, you say "Okay I'll start working on it", they don't hear from you for a week, they start to regret the purchase.

**Fix:** Immediate onboarding sequence:
1. Payment received → Auto-email: "Welcome! Here's what happens next..."
2. Within 4 hours → Loom video: "Hey [Name], just got your payment! Starting your build today. I'll send you the first draft in 48 hours. In the meantime, here's what I need from you: [list]"
3. Day 2 → Update email: "Making great progress! Here's a sneak peek [screenshot]"
4. Day 3-4 → Deliver draft, schedule review call
5. Week 2 → Go live
6. Month 1 → Performance report

This keeps them engaged, prevents buyer's remorse, and builds trust.

### 7. One-Time Projects Instead of Recurring
**Mistake:** Charge $5K to build a website, deliver it, relationship ends. Now you need to find a new $5K client every month.

**Fix:** Always structure as recurring:
- "Website build is $2,500 one-time, but includes first 3 months of hosting and updates ($500/mo value). After that, we'll continue monthly for ongoing optimization, updates, and support at $500/mo."

### Business Health Metrics
- **MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue):** Total monthly subscription revenue
- **YoY MRR Growth Rate:** Month-over-month increase %
- **Churn Rate:** Clients lost per month / total clients (target: <5%)
- **LTV (Lifetime Value):** Average revenue per client over lifetime (1/churn rate × monthly price)
- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost):** Ad spend + time cost to acquire one client  
- **CAC Payback Period:** Months to recover acquisition cost (target: <3 months)

**Example:**
- 10 clients × $997/mo = $9,970 MRR
- Churn rate: 1 client/year = 0.83%/month (insanely good)
- LTV: 1/0.0083 = 120 months × $997 = $119,640 per client
- CAC: $0 (organic outreach) + 4 hours @ $100/hour = $400
- CAC payback: $400 / $997 = 0.4 months (paid back in 2 weeks)

With these metrics, you'd know:
- It costs you $400 and 4 hours to get a client
- That client pays you ~$120K over their lifetime
- You recoup acquisition cost in 2 weeks
